# Homepage Narrative Outline

## Goal

The homepage should feel less like a marketing landing page and more like a high-resolution self-introduction. The reader should leave with a clear sense of who Yuzheng is, how he thinks, why his path is coherent, and where to go next if they want to learn from or work with him.

The core message:

> I study how people and organizations make better decisions in complex systems, then turn those decisions into products, growth, systems, and AI-native capabilities.

This is broader and more personal than "AI educator" or "course founder." AI-Builders, enterprise training, interviews, and content should appear as expressions of that throughline, not as separate product pitches.

## Narrative Spine

### 1. Opening: Who I Am

Job: establish the person, not the offer.

Draft direction:

> I'm Yuzheng Sun. I have spent my career trying to understand one question: how do people and organizations make better decisions in complex systems?
>
> That question has taken me through economics, experimentation, data science, product growth, management, AI, and education. The fields changed, but the obsession stayed the same: build a better model of reality, test it against the world, and turn what you learn into something useful.

Chinese direction:

> 我是孙煜征。我长期关心的不是某一个工具或风口，而是一个更底层的问题：人在复杂系统里，如何形成更好的判断，并把判断变成真实的产品、增长、组织能力和个人能力。

Notes:

- Do not lead with "300K followers" or "AI course."
- The first screen can still include credibility tokens, but they should sit underneath the thesis.
- The tone should be reflective, not salesy.

### 2. Throughline: The Work Behind the View

Job: turn resume into intellectual trajectory.

Current resume facts are strong but feel stacked. Rewrite them as what each environment taught him:

- Cornell economics: incentives, equilibrium, causal reasoning.
- Amazon economist: decision-making under business constraints.
- Meta data science: experimentation, A/B testing, growth, product judgment.
- Tencent IEG: management, data/AI org-building, operating inside a large system.
- Statsig: experimentation infrastructure, product growth, startup execution, AI frontier proximity.
- Superlinear / AI-Builders: teaching these patterns to people and teams.

Draft section title options:

- "The path that shaped my model"
- "How the thread developed"
- "From economics to experimentation to AI"

Important addition:

Growth and A/B testing should be explicit here. They are not minor career details. They are the bridge between "world model" and "action." They show how Yuzheng learned to convert opinions into observable facts.

### 3. What I Believe

Job: show worldview in a curated way. This should be the emotional center of the homepage.

Use 5 principles, distilled from the axioms. Keep them concrete and not too grand.

#### Better world models compound

The highest-leverage thing a person can improve is not raw effort or information intake, but the quality of the causal model they use to understand reality.

#### Strong opinions should invite better counterarguments

The point of having a strong view is not to win the argument. It is to make the idea sharp enough to be tested, challenged, and improved.

#### Experiments beat opinions

In ambiguous domains, the question should move from "who is right?" to "what small experiment would create a fact we can all look at?" This connects A/B testing, growth, Statsig, and AI-native prototyping.

#### The future belongs to builders, not users

AI makes access cheap. The advantage moves to people who can turn tools into workflows, products, systems, and assets.

#### Code is getting cheaper; judgment is getting more expensive

AI-native work is not about writing more code for its own sake. It is about using cheap code, throwaway tools, observability, and prototypes to buy expensive clarity.

Optional sixth, if needed:

#### Distribution is part of the product

Substance matters, but value that cannot be understood, trusted, or adopted does not fully exist in the world. Narrative and distribution are not vanity; they are how useful things travel.

### 4. What I Build

Job: introduce current work as a consequence of the worldview.

Do not present this as "my products." Present it as "where the thinking turned into practice."

Structure:

- AI-Builders: for individuals who want to move from user to builder.
- Enterprise training/advisory: for teams that want to move from AI demos to AI-native workflows and experimentation culture.
- Superlinear community: a peer lab where people practice, share artifacts, and update their models together.

Possible section title:

- "Where this becomes practice"
- "What I am building now"
- "From ideas to training grounds"

Copy direction:

> AI-Builders is not meant to be a course about prompts. It is a training ground for people who want to turn AI into shipped work.
>
> Enterprise training is not a keynote about AI trends. It is a way to help teams build the context, experiments, and workflow habits required for AI to survive contact with real work.

### 5. Rooms That Update My Model

Job: use the English network / guests materials without making them look like status display.

Current "Selected rooms" section should be reframed. The point is not "look who I know." The point is:

> I actively put myself in rooms where data, AI, product, capital, and engineering judgment collide.

Section title options:

- "Rooms that update my model"
- "Conversations that sharpen the work"
- "Where the model gets tested"

Cards should stay curated and ordered. The card copy should emphasize what each conversation represents:

- Mike Vernal: product, investing, AI, Facebook/Sequoia/Conviction perspective.
- Acquired: company-building and business history.
- Gergely Orosz: engineering execution and technology careers.
- AI Analytics: AI's effect on analytics across DoorDash, Gen, Anthropic, OpenAI, Lyft.
- Mike Develin: analytics leadership and Instagram/Google data perspective.
- DoorDash / Pinterest / Amazon: applied AI and analytics inside serious operating environments.
- Causal inference debate / John Meakin: experimentation and causal judgment.
- Cassie / Tim Chan: decision intelligence and data leadership.
- Yale: AI and education.

This section can be visually rich, but the framing should be quiet and intellectual.

### 6. If You Want to Go Deeper

Job: route visitors without overwhelming them.

This is where the current YouTube playlists, guest directory, course links, community links, and book can go.

Possible structure:

- Learn from my writing/videos
- Explore long-form conversations
- Build with AI-Builders
- Bring this into your organization

The homepage should not try to show every playlist and every proof item. It should give enough direction for different visitor types.

## Recommended Page Order

1. Hero: thesis + quiet credibility
2. Throughline: economics -> experimentation -> growth -> AI
3. What I believe: 5 principles
4. What I build: AI-Builders / enterprise / community
5. Rooms that update my model: selected conversations and talks
6. Proof from students and teams: reviews, student count, company sessions
7. Deeper paths: YouTube, guests, book, community, contact

## What To Reduce From Current Homepage

- Reduce repeated marketing claims like "rigorous AI course" unless they serve a narrative point.
- Reduce card-heavy sections that all look equally important.
- Move broad playlist grids lower or compress them into "Explore my content."
- Avoid making guest/network cards feel like a trophy wall.
- Avoid section titles that sound like product pages when the page is trying to be personal.

## What To Add

- A concise personal thesis.
- A real professional growth arc.
- Explicit experimentation / A/B testing / growth language.
- A convictions section that feels like Yuzheng, not generic AI thought leadership.
- A softer, more narrative bridge between personal background and commercial work.

This is only a working draft. The final copy should be sharper and more personal, but this direction is closer to a narrative homepage than the current marketing-page structure.

## Key Decision Before Implementation

Before editing the homepage, decide how personal the tone should be:

1. Conservative: polished professional narrative, less vulnerable.
2. Medium: personal throughline + selected convictions + some reflective language.
3. Strong: more like a public intellectual homepage, including self-critique and unresolved questions.

Recommended: Medium. It gives the page a real human voice without making it too essay-like or too informal.
